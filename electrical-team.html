Understand the behavior of our razor inertial measurement unit (IMU) and our depth sensor, characterize and calibrate the sensors, and develop the code that enables the sensors to interpret the data in a useful manner for our control system and mission logic.

We used 3 sensors for the system:

  • 9 Degrees of Freedom - Razor IMU:
    • 3-axis gyroscope
    • 3-axis accelerometer
  • Bar 30 Pressure Sensor

The Bar 30 sensor communicates with an Arduino microcontroller via I2C Communication using the SDA (Data) and SCL (Clocking) pins.

Digital signal processing (DSP) of the signals captured through our hydrophone array, as well as develop an instrumentation channel to be printed on PCB as well as code for DSP microcontrollers to be able to locate the frequencies in the robosub competition.

In order to achieve our goals, we have:

  • Used Instrumentation Amplifier
    • Due to the hydrophone's low output voltage, the signal needs to be amplified
  • Designed 4th-Order Butterworth Filter
    • Due to the noisy underwater environment, the raw signal needs to be filtered in order to obtain a clean one
  • Implemented Ultra-Short Baseline Method
    • Due to how critical the hydrophone's position is for the mathematical analysis of the signals, the best method needs to be chosen

Create all the circuits in order to distribute the power in different voltages to the different components, regulate voltages, develop a kill switch to turn off the AUV in case of emergency, as well as safely handle, store and utilize the LiPo batteries in our AUV.

For choosing the correct battery we went with the A123 26650. From battery parameters, these batteries have a high discharge/charge rate with better efficiency and long life cicles. Also a high 20C rating of discharge.

To determine the requirements of a the battery pack:

  • For a 30 minute maximum run time: Ah = (12.5 A)*(4motors)*(0.5h) = 25Ah

The distribution of power was done using TIâ€™s LM2678 buck converters. The converters used where from 13.2V to 5V and 9V. This converters need a circuit designed to stabilize the output voltage.

Safety measures are part of the requirements of using high voltage/currents batteries. A kill switch circuit was design in order to cut power to the motors in case batteries fall under the recommended voltage.

EagleCAD was used in order to design the PCB for the kill switch. A schematic and board layouts were made with all the necessary files that manufacturers need to print the PCB were generated.
